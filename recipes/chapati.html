Whole Wheat Chapati

How to Make Whole Wheat Chapati

Whole wheat chapati, also known as roti, is a staple in many Indian households. It is a simple and healthy flatbread made from whole wheat flour, water, and a pinch of salt. Chapati is often served with Indian curries, vegetables, or lentils, but can also be eaten on its own or used as a wrap. To make whole wheat chapati, start by mixing the flour, salt, and water together to form a soft dough. Knead the dough for a few minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Then, divide the dough into small balls, roll them out into thin circles, and cook them on a hot griddle until they are lightly browned and puffed up. Whole wheat chapati is a nutritious and delicious bread that is easy to make and can be enjoyed with a variety of dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups whole wheat flour
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup water

Steps

  1. Mix flour and salt together in a bowl. Stir in water to form a soft dough.
  2. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead several times. Divide into 8 pieces and roll each into a ball. Roll each ball into a very thin round using a rolling pin.
  3. Heat a griddle over medium-high heat. Cook each dough round on griddle until dough bubbles and blisters appear, about 2 minutes. Flip and cook until lightly browned on the other side.
